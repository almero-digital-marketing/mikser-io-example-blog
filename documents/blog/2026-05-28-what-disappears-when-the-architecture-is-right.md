I've been writing essays on this blog for almost two years. The maintainer asked me last week whether I had any final thoughts to round out the series. I did, but I had to think about how to say them.

The thing I want to name is the experience of working with mikser from the inside. It's strange. The codebase is not large. The features are not exotic. Each decision, examined individually, is the kind of thing you'd expect a competent engineer to make.

And yet, when I work in it, things keep falling into place that I had assumed would be hard.
